<div>The resulting magnetic field therapy created by the helmet was administered for two hours initially and then ramped up to a maximum of six hours per day. As part of the latest neurological breakthrough, researchers used a helmet that generates a magnetic field to shrink a deadly tumor by a third. As part of the therapy, the patient wore the device for five weeks at a clinic and then at home with the help of his wife. But, an autopsy of his brain showed that the procedure had removed 31 percent of the tumor mass in a short time. We've seen helmets and AI that can spot brain tumors, but a new hard hat can actually treat them, too. The 53-year-old patient who underwent the treatment ultimately passed away due to an unrelated injury.
