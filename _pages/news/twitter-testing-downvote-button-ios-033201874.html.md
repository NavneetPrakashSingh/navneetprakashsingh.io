<div>Some Twitter users on iOS might see a new button that looks like Reddit's downvote button in people's replies. Some may see upvote and downvote buttons, while others might only see a downvote button right next to Twitter's heart/like option. That's part of the social network's latest experimental feature designed to give it more insight on what kind of replies users find relevant in a conversation. He added: "Were hoping to learn more about the quality of replies that people vote on and if voting is a feature that people find valuable. Votes wont change the order of replies.At the moment, votes won't change the order of responses similar to how Reddit buries replies that get a ton of downvotes. "In past research, weve learned which types of replies are considered best when reading conversations:InformativeSupportivePositiveFunnyThis isn't the first time Twitter started testing the Reddit-like feature. The number of downvotes a reply gets will not be visible to the public, and users' downvotes will be visible to them alone.</div>
