<div>The company is also getting closer to switching over to its new 4680 battery cells, having successfully validated the battery tech's "performance and lifetime" at its California-based fabrication facility. Tesla CEO Elon Musk also revealed more details about the company's plan to open its Supercharger network to other EV brands later this year. "After selling over a million vehicles equipped with radar, we have collected enough data to start removing it in some regions," the company wrote in its shareholder deck. In terms of tech, Tesla's use of radar as part of the vehicle's Full Self Driving system will soon be coming to an end. "The removal of radar, which is enabled by our collection of a vast dataset of corner cases, allows us to focus on vision and increase the pace of improvement." Musk said, warning that the issue will likely not be resolved before the end of this year. He did note, however, that the ongoing global shortage of microchips remains an cantraining force on vehicle production.</div>
