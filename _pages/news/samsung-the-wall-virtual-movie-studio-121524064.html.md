<div>Samsung says its massive screen measures over 1,000 inches and supports up to 16K high-resolution content.But, the Korean company hasn't embraced virtual production quite like Sony. The emergence of virtual productions like those popularized by Disney's The Mandalorian have given display giants a new niche for their massive screens. As a result, virtual production is expected to become the norm in filmmaking within five years, according to an industry report by the Gteborg Film Festival. Sony, which boasts a movie production arm, is already using its modular crystal LEDs to provide backgrounds for shoots. The company will install an oval-shaped main display at CJ ENM's new complex with a diameter of 20 metres and a height of seven metres or more. Samsung says its latest screens will deliver improved visuals thanks to their support for HDR 10+ and optimized frame rates such as 23.976Hz, 29.97Hz and 59.94Hz. Virtual productions are gaining traction in global filmmaking.</div>
