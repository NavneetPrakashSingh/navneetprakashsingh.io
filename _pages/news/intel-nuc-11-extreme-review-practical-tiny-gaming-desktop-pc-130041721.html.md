<div>I couldn't fathom why anyone would pay out the nose for a compromised desktop that couldn't fit a full-sized GPU. Given the small workable space, I was also interested in seeing how more powerful GPUs would fit into the NUC 11. Its black metal case sports mesh air vents along the sides, giving you a peek at the GPU within and three large case fans up top. But I couldn't review a gaming PC without actually playing some games, could I? I was even able to get Control running in 1440p between 60 and 70 fps with medium ray tracing settings and graphics set to high. )This being the ultimate DIY PC kit, I also had to tear it open to see just how modular it actually was. Hit the power button and it springs to life with an LED skull along the front panel, as well as underside LED lighting.</div>
