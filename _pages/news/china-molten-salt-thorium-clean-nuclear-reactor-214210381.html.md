<div>In theory, China can extend its political influence without contributing to nuclear arms proliferation.That might worry the US and other political rivals that are behind on thorium reactors. Are you intrigued by the possibility of using nuclear reactors to curb emissions, but worried about their water use and long-term safety? Conventional uranium reactors produce waste that stays extremely radioactive for up to 10,000 years, requiring lead containers and extensive security. A full-scale commercial reactor should be ready by 2030.The technology should not only be kinder to the environment, but mitigate some political controversy. The technology doesn't require water, and can't easily be used to produce nuclear weapons. The waste also includes plutonium-239, an isotope crucial to nuclear weapons. If there is a leak, the molten salt cools enough that it effectively seals in the thorium and prevents significant leaks.</div>
