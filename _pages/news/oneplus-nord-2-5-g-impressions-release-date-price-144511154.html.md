<div>While its OnePlus highest specification front sensor, capable of taking 32-megapixel pictures, its just a single camera sensor, compared to the dual-array on the original Nord. The rear module is now three cameras, not four, with a primary 50-megapixel (up from 48MP) sensor with optical image stabilization (OIS) responsible for the heavy lifting. Video in low light suffered from a weird pulsing effect, that might be due to electronic image stabilization. It uses electronic image stabilization to amp up how much wobble it can handle, and video was pleasantly smooth  if the lighting was good enough. Its called the MediaTek Dimensity 1200-AI, which, unsurprisingly, offers a degree of AI-centric muscle to processes like photo manipulation and the aforementioned video playback boosts. Ive been testing the Nord 2 for a couple of days, and if you liked the proposition of the original Nord, youll probably like its sequel. While it might look the part, is it as good?I loved the vivid eye-catching sky blue of last years Nord, but its successor has a more restrained palette.</div>
