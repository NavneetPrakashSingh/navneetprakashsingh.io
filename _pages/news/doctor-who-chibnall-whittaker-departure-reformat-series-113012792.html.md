<div>Every few years, often as the shows creative team changed, it would become an almost entirely different show. You could argue that this lack of sentimentality has been the case since the shows first mission-switch, which happened in its fifth episode. Chibnall also deserves credit for hiring the first two writers of color in the shows nearly sixty year history. The fact that many of the episodes had an explicit focus on material social history suggested a bright new direction for the series. That would be a mistake, because Who is at its best when it pushes away from whatever genre show is cresting into the mainstream that year. And Whittakers departure before she could work with another executive producer will be yet another tragically wasted opportunity in this era. A trio of specials through next year would herald the pairs departure from the long-running series.</div>
