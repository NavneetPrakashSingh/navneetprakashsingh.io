Xiaomi's average selling prices are well below those of Samsung (40 percent) and Apple (75 percent), and even flagships like the Mi 11 are relatively affordable. Samsung held on to its lead at 19 percent, but there's clearly not much of a gap.The key, as you might guess, was price. The second quarter is historically weak for Apple as it reaches the tail end of a given iPhone's release cycle. While Xiaomi has long had a major presence in its native China, the company has had a much tougher fight for international recognition. And unlike its fallen competitor, Huawei, Xiaomi doesn't have a US blacklisting to cloud its future.It's not certain that Xiaomi will hold on to its position. Canalys also notes that Oppo and Vivo aren't staying still they have their own dreams of world dominance.
